Overview

GRATS (Granular Rate Adaptation for Temporal Scaling) is a naturally recorded parallel multi-speaker Mandarin dataset designed for benchmarking Speech Time-Scale Modification (STSM) systems.

Unlike evaluation setups that use artificially time-scaled references, GRATS provides independently recorded target-rate utterances from the same speaker and sentence. This enables direct comparison between STSM-generated outputs and authentic target-rate recordings.

Benchmark

GRATS is designed for evaluating STSM systems using natural target-rate references. The benchmark includes representative classical and neural STSM baselines and complementary metrics covering intelligibility, perceptual quality, temporal consistency, and pitch consistency.

  • Character Error Rate (CER)
  • Perceptual Evaluation of Speech Quality (PESQ)
  • Short-Time Objective Intelligibility (STOI)
  • Deep Noise Suppression Mean Opinion Score (DNSMOS)
  • Syllable-level duration Mean Absolute Error (MAE)
  • Fundamental frequency (F0) correlation

Benchmark scripts, metadata format, and evaluation instructions will be made available on this page.
